About Me

I'm currently a Master's student in the PSI program at the Perimeter Institute, where I'm enjoying being immersed in theoretical physics coursework and exciting research. Fascinated by the dynamics of entanglement in systems at the interface of quantum information and condensed matter, I'm eager to apply both analytical and computational tools to better understand such systems.

Research

Over the past five years, I've conducted research at the Perimeter Institute, Pomona College, TRIUMF, and the University of British Columbia. There, I've investigated topics in condensed matter, gravity, particle physics, quantum information, and quantum optics.

Transitions in Quantum Complexity

Clifford circuits and universal circuits represent two regimes of unitary evolution: random Clifford circuits produce more ordered, reversible behaviour, whereas random universal circuits quickly scramble states in very complex ways. Between these two regimes, an interesting transition in quantum complexity occurs, which I am studying this transition under the supervision of Professor Alioscia Hamma.

Resolving Time Travel Paradoxes

Einstein's equations admit solutions with closed timelike curves, allowing for the possibility of time travel — and the danger of time travel paradoxes. In a paper with Professor Barak Shoshany, we resolve such paradoxes while simultaneously allowing for interesting new phenomena by extending our spacetimes to include multiple histories. Our paper was also featured in an article by New Scientist magazine.
