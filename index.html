Research

Over the past five years, I've conducted research at the Perimeter Institute, Pomona College, TRIUMF, and the University of British Columbia. There, I've investigated topics in condensed matter, gravity, particle physics, quantum information, and quantum optics.

Transitions in Quantum Complexity

Clifford circuits and universal circuits represent two regimes of unitary evolution: random Clifford circuits produce more ordered, reversible behaviour, whereas random universal circuits quickly scramble states in very complex ways. Between these two regimes, in mostly-Clifford circuits doped with non-Clifford gates, an interesting transition in quantum complexity occurs.

Resolving Time Travel Paradoxes

Einstein's equations admit solutions with closed timelike curves, allowing for the possibility of time travel — and the danger of time travel paradoxes. Extending our spacetimes to include multiple histories can resolve such paradoxes while simultaneously allowing for interesting new phenomena.
